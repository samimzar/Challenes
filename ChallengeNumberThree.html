Camelids

The four llamas and two cammels are camelidsds tend to be large and are strictly herbiovorous. Camelids differ from ruminants in several ways. They have three-chambered rather than a four-chambered digestive tract; an upper lip that is split in two with each part sparately mobile; and an isolated incisor in the upper jaw. They have long legs that, because they lack tensor skin to bridge between thigh and body, look longer still.(from Wikipedia)
